Our Story

KATHA was born from the belief that every handmade thing carries a part of the maker's soul — and that these stories deserve to be shared.

Where KATHA Began

The "KATHA Project" was created by three innovative students from Bicol University Polangui as a showcase of their creativity and dedication to artisan craft. What started as a class project quickly evolved into a platform to explore and share unique handmade creations that reflect their skills and imagination.

Through this project, the students demonstrate how traditional techniques and modern ideas can merge, bringing fresh perspectives to design and craftsmanship. Each creation tells a story of creativity, teamwork, and learning.

Today, the project highlights a range of handcrafted works — from woven items to decorative pieces — celebrating the talent and vision of young artisans in the Bicol region.

Our Values

Handmade, Always

No machines. No mass production. Every item is made entirely by hand, carrying the unique fingerprint of its maker.

Sustainably Sourced

We use only natural, locally-sourced materials — rattan, abaca, shells, clay — harvested responsibly to protect our environment.

Artisan First

Fair wages, fair trade, and fair recognition. Every artisan behind KATHA is credited, compensated, and celebrated.
